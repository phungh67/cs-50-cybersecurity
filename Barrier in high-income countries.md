## Tailored health education and communication

There are some barriers that caused directly by the illiterate or the "cultural" different. These obstacles can come from "mindset", example: fear of discovering illness, or some religious reasons. With these evidences, there is a research and also proof that "tailored" screening program can show effective. Also, personalized and accessible communication has proven effective in increasing cancer screening.
Several methods were given out to overcome the barriers which prevents women from taking appropriate early screening:
- Culturally tailored health education and communication.
- Trust building initiatives with providers and health system.
- Family and community support for acculturation and engagement.
- Awareness and knowledge on increased risk perception.

>[!Problem]
>Additionally, migrants, refugees, non-English-speaking individuals, and those recently arrived have low participation rates in breast, cervical, and colorectal cancer screening compared to the general populations. Low screening rates were attributed to factors like lack of awareness, cultural stigma, invasive procedures, and systemic barriers including insurance gaps, language difficulties, and discouraging cultural beliefs. This stark equity gaps of the uptake of screening services among migrants and general populations suggests need for focussed service interventions towards universal access to screening services.
