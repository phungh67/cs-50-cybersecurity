Welcome to the Real-Time Systems module. While this might not be the primary focus of my day-to-day architectural work, the mechanics of deterministic computing and strict-timing environments are undeniably critical (and quite satisfying to map out). 

This directory skips the lengthy theoretical essays and gets straight to the point: it is heavily focused on **practical application, problem-sets, and exercises**. 

## 📖 What's Inside?

The notes and exercises here are designed to drill down into the mathematical logic that keeps real-time systems running without failure. Expect to find practice problems covering:

* **Task Scheduling Algorithms:** Practical calculations and scenarios for evaluating how tasks are managed by the CPU.
* **Rate Monotonic Scheduling (RMS):** Drilling into static priority assignments, preemption, and working through the Liu and Layland utilization bounds to prove system schedulability.
* **Timing Constraints:** Evaluating system responsiveness, hard vs. soft deadlines, and calculating worst-case execution times (WCET).

## 🛠️ Why Keep This?

Even if your primary focus is DevOps or Cloud architecture, understanding how real-time scheduling algorithms prioritize computational resources at the lowest levels offers a much sharper perspective on system performance, resource starvation, and overall reliability.
